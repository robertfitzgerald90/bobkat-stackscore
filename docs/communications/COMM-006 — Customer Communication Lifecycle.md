# 1. Purpose

This document defines the customer communication philosophy, workflow standards, lifecycle events, and delivery requirements used throughout StackScore.

Customer communications are intended to keep organizations informed throughout their technology improvement journey while reinforcing the professionalism and consistency of the Bobkat IT Standard.

Every communication should:

- Deliver meaningful business value
- Explain the customer's current stage
- Clearly communicate the next recommended action
- Maintain a consistent visual identity
- Build trust in the Bobkat IT consulting process

This document governs all automated and manually initiated customer communications.

---

# 2. Scope

This document applies to:

- Transactional emails
- Assessment communications
- Roadmap notifications
- Proposal notifications
- Project notifications
- Quarterly technology review communications
- Account management emails
- Customer invitations
- Future SMS notifications
- Future in-application notifications
- Future push notifications

Marketing newsletters are outside the scope of this document.

---

# 3. Communication Philosophy

Every communication should answer four questions.

## Why am I receiving this?

Clearly explain the purpose of the communication.

## What happened?

Describe the event that triggered the notification.

## Why does it matter?

Explain the business impact using executive-friendly language.

## What should I do next?

Provide one primary call-to-action.

Every communication should have a single, obvious next step.

---

# 4. Communication Principles

## Professional

Communications should resemble enterprise SaaS platforms while remaining approachable.

## Business Focused

Avoid technical jargon whenever possible.

Focus on outcomes rather than technology.

## Action Oriented

Every communication should encourage meaningful progress.

## Consistent

Visual design, terminology, branding, and structure should remain consistent across every communication.

## Secure

Sensitive information should never be exposed directly within email.

Protected resources should always require authentication.

# 6. Standard Email Structure

Every customer email should contain the following sections.

## Header

- StackScore branding
- Powered by Bobkat IT

---

## Hero Section

Large headline describing the event.

Example:

Your Technology Assessment is Complete

---

## Introduction

Brief explanation of why the recipient is receiving the email.

---

## Summary

Provide a concise overview of the related business event.

---

## Primary Call-to-Action

One prominent action button.

Examples:

- View Assessment
- Review Roadmap
- Review Proposal
- Reset Password

---

## Supporting Information

Relevant secondary details.

---

## Footer

Support information

Company information

Legal links

Powered by Bobkat IT

---

# 7. Authentication Standards

Protected customer resources must require authentication.

Examples:

- Assessment Results
- Technology Blueprint
- Roadmaps
- Proposals
- Projects

If a user is not authenticated:

Email Link

↓

Login

↓

Redirect Back

The original destination should be preserved.

---

# 8. Recipient Management

Before publishing customer-facing deliverables, consultants should be able to select recipients.

Supported recipient types:

- Primary Contact
- Assessment Owner
- Executive Contact
- Organization Users
- Additional Email Addresses
- Consultant
- Internal Bobkat IT Users

Recipient selections should be stored with the communication history.

---

# 9. Attachments

Communications may optionally include generated documents.

Supported attachments include:

- Executive Reports
- Assessment Reports
- Proposal PDFs
- Project Summaries
- Roadmap PDFs

Attachments should always represent the currently published version.

---

# 10. Call-to-Action Standards

Every communication should include one primary CTA.

Examples:

Assessment Complete

→ View Assessment Results

Roadmap Ready

→ View Roadmap

Proposal Ready

→ Review Proposal

Password Reset

→ Reset Password

Project Completed

→ View Project

Primary actions should never overwhelm the user with multiple competing choices.

- Sent
- Delivered
- Opened
- Clicked

# 12. Customer Timeline Integration

Meaningful communications should appear within the customer timeline.

Examples:

Assessment Invitation Sent

Assessment Completed

Roadmap Published

Proposal Published

Proposal Approved

Project Created

Project Completed

Quarterly Review Scheduled

Timeline entries should tell the complete story of the customer relationship.

---

# 13. Quarterly Review Philosophy

Quarterly Technology Reviews are consultant-driven.

StackScore should not automatically email customers when a quarterly review becomes due.

Instead:

Quarterly Review Due

↓

Internal Reminder

↓

Consultant Review

↓

Manual Customer Invitation

This ensures customer expectations align with consultant availability.

---

# 14. Approval Workflows

Proposal approvals should follow a controlled workflow.

Proposal Published

↓

Customer Reviews Proposal

↓

Customer Approves or Requests Changes

↓

Bobkat IT Notification

↓

Proposal Status Updated

Customers should never be presented with a "Reject Proposal" action.

Instead, allow:

Approve

or

Request Changes

---

# 15. Communication Ownership

Every communication should have an owner.

Possible owners include:

- System
- Consultant
- Organization Administrator
- Customer

Ownership determines who is responsible for initiating, approving, or managing the communication.

---

# 16. Branding Standards

Every communication must:

- Follow StackScore branding
- Reinforce Bobkat IT as the consulting organization
- Use consistent typography
- Use consistent spacing
- Maintain accessibility standards
- Support desktop and mobile rendering
