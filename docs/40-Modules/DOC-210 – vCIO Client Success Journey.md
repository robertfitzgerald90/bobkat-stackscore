# DOC - 210 – vCIO Client Success Journey

**Status:** Planned  
**Priority:** High  
**Owner:** Bobkat IT  
**Module:** StackScore Client Portal

---

# Objective

Design a premium client onboarding and success experience that transforms StackScore from an assessment platform into an ongoing strategic technology advisory platform.

The objective is to ensure every subscriber feels like they have hired a trusted technology advisor—not simply purchased software.

The experience should minimize redundant data entry, adapt to the customer's existing relationship with Bobkat IT, and provide a clear roadmap for long-term success.

---

# Vision

When a client subscribes to StackScore vCIO, they should immediately feel that Bobkat IT already understands their business and has a plan.

Instead of:

> "Welcome to your dashboard."

The experience should communicate:

> "Welcome. We've already started planning your technology future."

---

# Guiding Principles

## Adaptive Onboarding

Never ask a client for information that StackScore already knows.

The onboarding workflow should adapt based on the customer's current relationship.

---

## Progressive Disclosure

Only collect information when it becomes valuable.

Avoid long onboarding forms.

Clients should never feel overwhelmed.

---

## Guided Success

Every screen should answer one question:

> "What should I do next?"

The platform should always provide a recommended next step.

---

## Relationship First

StackScore is not software.

StackScore is the digital extension of Bobkat IT's consulting practice.

The portal should reinforce that relationship throughout the customer lifecycle.

# Full Onboarding Experience

## Step 1

Welcome

Explain:

- What StackScore vCIO is
- What happens next
- What the first strategy session will accomplish

---

## Step 2

Company Profile

Collect:

- Company Name
- Website
- Industry
- Locations
- Employees
- Technology Users
- Estimated Endpoints

---

## Step 3

Contacts

Collect:

- Executive Contact
- Technology Contact
- Billing Contact

---

## Step 4

Current Technology

Collect high-level information only.

Examples:

- Microsoft 365
- Google Workspace
- Firewall Vendor
- Switch Vendor
- Wi-Fi Vendor
- Backup Solution
- Endpoint Management
- Antivirus

Do not ask implementation-level questions during onboarding.

---

## Step 5

Business Goals

Examples:

- Biggest technology frustrations
- Current initiatives
- Growth plans
- Compliance concerns
- Budget planning preferences

This information should become the foundation for the technology roadmap.

---

## Step 6

Schedule Strategy Session

Redirect to Cal.com.

Completion of onboarding should not require immediate scheduling, but it should be strongly encouraged.

# Quarterly Business Reviews

Every 90 days StackScore should guide both consultant and client through a structured review.

Deliverables:

- Executive Summary
- Technology Score
- Score Movement
- Completed Projects
- Remaining Risks
- Open Recommendations
- Budget Summary
- Planned Investments
- Next Quarter Priorities

The review should generate a polished PDF suitable for executive leadership.

---

# Long-Term Vision

StackScore should evolve beyond a technology assessment platform.

The long-term vision is to become the operational hub for every Bobkat IT client relationship, combining:

- Technology Assessments
- Strategic Planning
- Roadmap Management
- Quarterly Business Reviews
- Executive Reporting
- Project Tracking
- Budget Planning
- Client Collaboration
- Technology Lifecycle Management

The end goal is for clients to view StackScore as the central platform for managing the health, strategy, and continuous improvement of their technology environment.
