# DOC-204 – Technology Investment Roadmap Framework

**Document Owner:** Bobkat IT  
**Product:** StackScore  
**Status:** Draft  
**Version:** 1.0

---

# Purpose

The Technology Investment Roadmap provides a structured, multi-phase strategy for improving an organization's technology over time.

Rather than presenting a list of disconnected projects, the Roadmap organizes technology investments into logical phases that align with business objectives, organizational priorities, available budgets, and long-term technology strategy.

The Roadmap represents the strategic direction of the client's Technology Program.

---

# Vision

Technology should not evolve through isolated purchases or reactive projects.

Technology should evolve through intentional investment.

The Technology Investment Roadmap provides a visual representation of where the organization is today, where it intends to go, and how it will get there.

Every Project should contribute toward a larger organizational objective.

---

# Philosophy

Organizations already understand investment portfolios.

Technology should be managed in the same way.

Every Project represents an investment.

Every investment should produce measurable value.

Every phase should build upon previous investments.

The Roadmap transforms technical work into a long-term business strategy.

---

# Objectives

The Technology Investment Roadmap exists to:

- Prioritize technology investments.
- Organize Projects into logical phases.
- Visualize long-term progress.
- Align investments with business objectives.
- Improve executive understanding.
- Support budgeting.
- Simplify technology planning.
- Reduce reactive decision making.

---

# Core Principles

## Projects Drive the Roadmap

Projects—not Recommendations—are scheduled within the Roadmap.

Recommendations justify Projects.

Projects deliver business outcomes.

---

## Investments Build Upon One Another

Technology improvements should follow a logical progression.

Earlier investments should strengthen future initiatives.

Dependencies should be respected whenever possible.

---

## Business Before Technology

Roadmap decisions should prioritize business outcomes.

Technology implementations exist to support organizational goals.

---

## Roadmaps Remain Flexible

Roadmaps are living strategic documents.

Business priorities change.

Technology evolves.

Roadmaps should evolve accordingly.

---

# Roadmap Structure

The Roadmap consists of multiple implementation phases.

Example:

```text
Technology Investment Roadmap

Phase 1

Security Foundation

↓

Phase 2

Infrastructure Modernization

↓

Phase 3

Business Enablement

↓

Phase 4

Optimization & Innovation

↓

Continuous Improvement
```

Phases should remain configurable.

---

# Phase Structure

Each Phase contains one or more Projects.

Example:

Phase 1

Security Foundation

Projects

- Identity Modernization
- Backup Modernization
- Firewall Replacement

Business Objective

Reduce organizational cyber risk.

Expected Technology Score

61 → 70

Estimated Investment

$42,000

Expected Duration

Q1–Q2

---

# Project Placement

Projects should be organized according to:

- Dependencies
- Business Priority
- Budget
- Risk Reduction
- Organizational Readiness
- Consultant Guidance

Projects should never be placed solely according to assessment score.

# Business Objectives

Every Phase should support one or more business objectives.

Examples:

- Improve Cybersecurity
- Improve Operational Resilience
- Support Company Growth
- Reduce Downtime
- Improve Compliance
- Modernize Infrastructure
- Standardize Technology
- Increase Productivity
- Improve Executive Visibility

Technology should always support business goals.

Examples:

Annual Budget

Projected Budget

Recurring Costs

Capital Investment

The objective is to support informed executive decision making.

---

# Relationship to Projects

Projects represent the implementation layer of the Roadmap.

**Scheduled** projects should:

- Belong to at most one active Phase.
- Support one or more business objectives.
- Produce measurable outcomes.
- Advance the Technology Program.

Projects **may exist without a phase** (program backlog / unscheduled). Membership rule is canonical in DOC-120A and DEV-002 Phase 4.

Projects remain independently manageable while contributing toward the larger strategy.

---

# Relationship to Technology Journey

The Roadmap defines future direction.

The Technology Journey records completed progress.

Roadmaps answer:

"Where are we going?"

Technology Journey answers:

"How did we get here?"

Together they provide complete lifecycle visibility.

---

# Relationship to Planning Workshop

The Planning Workshop creates the initial Technology Investment Roadmap.

Future Planning Workshops refine and optimize the Roadmap as business priorities evolve.

The Roadmap remains a living document.

---

Technology planning should feel approachable rather than technical.

# Visualization Principles

The Roadmap should emphasize progression rather than scheduling.

Potential visualizations include:

- Phase progression
- Technology maturity progression
- Investment timeline
- Business objective completion
- Program completion
- Risk reduction
- Initiative progress

Visual storytelling should be prioritized over detailed project scheduling.

---

# Future Opportunities

Potential future capabilities include:

- Drag-and-drop phase planning
- Budget forecasting
- AI-assisted roadmap generation
- Industry benchmark alignment
- Scenario planning
- Multi-year investment forecasting
- ROI projections
- Executive presentation mode
- Roadmap comparison
- Alternative investment strategies

---

# Design Principles

The Technology Investment Roadmap should:

- Feel strategic.
- Focus on business outcomes.
- Remain flexible.
- Encourage long-term thinking.
- Simplify executive decision making.
- Reduce reactive technology spending.
- Reinforce continuous improvement.

The Roadmap should never resemble traditional project management software.

---

# Success Criteria

The Roadmap succeeds when:

- Clients understand where they are going.
- Technology investments feel intentional.
- Projects support larger business objectives.
- Consultants can easily explain implementation order.
- Executive confidence increases.

---

# Future Vision

The Technology Investment Roadmap should become the central planning artifact of the client's Technology Program.

Rather than discussing isolated projects, consultants should guide clients through phased technology investments that continuously improve organizational capability.

Technology should become a managed business asset with a clear direction, measurable progress, and visible return on investment.

# Cursor Development Guidance

The Technology Investment Roadmap is a strategic planning artifact—not a project schedule.

Future development should prioritize executive understanding, business alignment, measurable progress, and investment visibility over traditional project management concepts.

Roadmaps should organize Projects into meaningful investment phases while continuously communicating organizational progress toward long-term technology goals.

Technology planning should always emphasize business value, strategic direction, and continuous improvement.
