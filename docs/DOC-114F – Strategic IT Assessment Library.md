# Purpose

The Strategic IT Assessment Library defines every assessment question used to evaluate how effectively technology aligns with the organization's business objectives, long-term planning, governance, and continuous improvement efforts.

The objective is to measure whether IT functions as a strategic business partner rather than solely as a technical support organization.

This document should be read in conjunction with:

* DOC-110 – Assessment Framework
* DOC-111 – Scoring Engine Specification
* DOC-112 – Recommendation Engine Specification
* DOC-114 – Assessment Library Specification

---

# Assessment Objective

The Strategic IT Assessment measures an organization's ability to plan, govern, budget, and continuously improve its technology environment.

Areas evaluated include:

* IT Strategy
* Business Alignment
* Technology Roadmaps
* IT Governance
* Budget Planning
* Lifecycle Management
* Technical Debt
* Standardization
* Risk Management
* Vendor Management
* Executive Reporting
* Continuous Improvement

# Strategic IT Maturity Philosophy

Technology should support and accelerate business objectives.

A mature IT organization proactively plans, budgets, standardizes, measures outcomes, and continuously improves its technology environment.

Strategic IT emphasizes long-term sustainability over short-term problem solving.

# Business Rules

* Strategic IT questions shall evaluate planning, governance, and alignment rather than technical implementations.
* Technology investments should be evaluated based on business value and measurable outcomes.
* Technical debt shall be identified and prioritized for remediation.
* Continuous improvement shall be considered a core component of technology maturity.
* All scoring shall be governed by DOC-111 – Scoring Engine Specification.
