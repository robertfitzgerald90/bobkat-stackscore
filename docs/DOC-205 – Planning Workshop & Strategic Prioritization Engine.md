# DOC-205 – Planning Workshop & Strategic Prioritization Engine

**Document Owner:** Bobkat IT  
**Product:** StackScore  
**Status:** Draft  
**Version:** 1.0

---

# Purpose

The Planning Workshop transforms assessment findings into a structured Technology Program tailored to the client's unique business objectives.

Technology Assessments identify opportunities.

The Planning Workshop determines which opportunities matter most to this specific organization.

This process represents the consultant's highest-value activity within StackScore.

---

# Vision

Technology recommendations should never be implemented simply because they exist.

Every technology investment should align with:

- Business objectives
- Risk tolerance
- Budget
- Organizational priorities
- Compliance requirements
- Operational constraints

The Planning Workshop converts technical findings into a practical multi-phase Technology Investment Roadmap.

---

# Philosophy

Technology consulting is not about recommending everything.

Technology consulting is about recommending the right things at the right time.

The Planning Workshop exists to prioritize technology investments that deliver the greatest business value.

Consultant expertise should always complement automated recommendations.

---

# Objectives

The Planning Workshop exists to:

- Understand the client's business.
- Prioritize technology investments.
- Define realistic implementation phases.
- Establish long-term strategic goals.
- Create measurable business outcomes.
- Align technology with executive priorities.

---

# Inputs

The Planning Workshop begins after a completed assessment.

Available inputs include:

- Assessment Results
- Technology Maturity
- Technology Pillar Scores
- Recommendations
- Risk Profile
- Existing Projects
- Historical Technology Journey

The consultant supplements this information through discussion with the client.

---

The Planning Workshop should guide discussion rather than function as a questionnaire.

Topics include:

## Business Goals

Examples:

- Growth
- Expansion

- Operational Efficiency
- Digital Transformation

## Business Challenges

Examples:

- Staffing limitations
- Aging infrastructure
- Compliance obligations

## Strategic Priorities

Examples:

- Improve security
- Improve productivity
- Support remote work

- Standardize technology

- Capital Budget
- Operating Budget
- Funding Constraints

# Strategic Prioritization

The Planning Workshop should never rely solely on technical risk.

Project prioritization should consider multiple factors.

Examples include:

- Technology Risk
- Business Impact
- Executive Priorities
- Budget
- Dependencies
- Compliance
- Operational Urgency
- Return on Investment
- Consultant Experience

Future versions may calculate a Strategic Priority Score.

---

# Planning Outputs

The Planning Workshop **configures** the existing Technology Program (created at first assessment) and **creates** the Technology Investment Roadmap.

Outputs:

- Configured Technology Program (goals, direction)
- Technology Investment Roadmap (versioned)
- Project Phases
- Immediate priorities
- Strategic Priorities
- Estimated Investment Timeline

The consultant retains final approval.

**Primary planning path** for new work. **TIP (DOC-103)** remains a legacy coexistence path during migration (DOC-120A §6). Do not auto-create projects from TIP phase labels.

---

# Consultant Override

Automation should assist—not replace—the consultant.

The consultant should always be able to:

- Change priorities
- Move Projects
- Delay implementation
- Accelerate initiatives
- Ignore recommendations
- Create custom Projects

Human expertise remains the final authority.

---

# Immediate Focus

Every Planning Workshop should identify a small number of immediate priorities.

The Immediate Focus represents:

"The most valuable work to complete next."

Immediate Focus should generally contain:

3–5 Projects.

The objective is clarity—not overwhelming the client.

---

# Relationship to Roadmap

The Planning Workshop creates the Technology Investment Roadmap.

Roadmaps should evolve over time.

Completed Projects naturally advance the organization through the Roadmap.

---

# Relationship to Projects

Projects become the implementation mechanism for the Planning Workshop.

Each Project should clearly support one or more business objectives identified during planning.

Projects without business alignment should be reconsidered.

---

# Relationship to Technology Journey

The Planning Workshop establishes the starting point for future Technology Journey milestones.

Every completed Project should demonstrate progress toward objectives established during planning.

---

# Executive Experience

Planning Workshops should feel collaborative.

Clients should feel they are making informed business decisions—not receiving technical instructions.

Technology should be discussed using business language whenever possible.

---

# Design Principles

Planning should:

- Encourage discussion.
- Remain flexible.
- Support executive decision making.
- Balance automation with consultant judgement.
- Prioritize measurable outcomes.
- Reduce unnecessary complexity.

Planning should never become a rigid workflow.

# Success Criteria

The Planning Workshop succeeds when:

- Technology investments align with business priorities.
- Clients understand why Projects were selected.
- Consultants can easily explain implementation order.
- Roadmaps feel realistic.
- Executive confidence increases.

---

# Future Vision

The Planning Workshop represents the transition between assessment and execution.

It transforms technical findings into strategic business initiatives.

Rather than simply fixing technology, consultants help organizations make informed investment decisions that improve capability over time.

This methodology should become one of the defining characteristics of StackScore.

# Cursor Development Guidance

The Planning Workshop is not a form.

It is a structured consulting methodology.

Future development should prioritize guided conversations, intelligent prioritization, consultant flexibility, and business alignment over rigid workflows.

The consultant should always remain the decision maker.

Automation exists to provide recommendations—not replace professional judgement.

Planning should produce a Technology Investment Roadmap that reflects the unique needs of each client rather than a generic sequence of technical improvements.
