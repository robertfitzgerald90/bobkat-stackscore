# Purpose

The Operations Assessment Library defines every assessment question used to evaluate the maturity, consistency, efficiency, and day-to-day management of an organization's IT operations.

The objective is to measure how effectively technology services are delivered, monitored, maintained, and continuously improved.

This document should be read in conjunction with:

* DOC-110 – Assessment Framework
* DOC-111 – Scoring Engine Specification
* DOC-112 – Recommendation Engine Specification
* DOC-114 – Assessment Library Specification

---

# Assessment Objective

The Operations Assessment measures the organization's ability to consistently operate and support its technology environment through standardized processes, proactive maintenance, and operational excellence.

Areas evaluated include:

* Endpoint Management
* Monitoring & Alerting
* Patch Management
* Asset Management
* Help Desk Operations
* Change Management
* Maintenance Processes
* Performance Monitoring
* Automation
* Reporting
* Operational Metrics
* Continuous Service Improvement

# Operations Maturity Philosophy

Technology operations should be proactive rather than reactive.

A mature operations program emphasizes visibility, standardization, automation, monitoring, preventive maintenance, and measurable service quality.

Operational maturity reduces downtime, improves consistency, and enables IT teams to focus on business value instead of constant firefighting.

# Business Rules

* Operations questions shall evaluate repeatable operational processes rather than individual technologies.
* Monitoring should emphasize actionable visibility over excessive alerting.
* Automation should reduce repetitive manual work while improving consistency.
* Operational maturity should be measured by proactive management rather than reactive support.
* All scoring shall be governed by DOC-111 – Scoring Engine Specification.
