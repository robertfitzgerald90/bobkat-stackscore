# Purpose

The Technology Maturity Engine (TME) is the core intelligence layer of StackScore. It governs how technology maturity is measured, projected, tracked, and improved over time.

Unlike traditional assessment platforms that generate a static score, StackScore is designed as a continuous technology improvement platform. The Technology Maturity Engine transforms assessments into living roadmaps that evolve as organizations implement recommendations and improve their environments.

This document defines the business rules that all future scoring, dashboards, reporting, recommendations, analytics, and customer progress tracking must follow.

---

# 1. Vision

StackScore is **not simply an assessment platform**.

It is a **Technology Maturity Management Platform**.

Every assessment establishes a baseline.

Every recommendation becomes an improvement opportunity.

Every completed initiative permanently advances the customer's technology maturity.

The customer dashboard should continuously demonstrate measurable progress throughout their engagement with BobKat IT.

---

# 2. Core Principles

The Technology Maturity Engine follows the principles below.

---

## 2.1 Technology Maturity is Dynamic

Technology maturity should never remain static after an assessment.

As recommendations are completed, maturity changes.

---

## 2.2 Recommendations Drive Improvement

Technology maturity increases only when improvements have actually been implemented.

Planning work does not improve the current maturity score.

---

## 2.3 Planned Work Predicts Future Maturity

Recommendations that have been approved or are actively being implemented contribute toward the projected maturity score.

---

## 2.4 Deferred Work is Excluded

Recommendations intentionally postponed should not contribute toward projected maturity.

This allows StackScore to represent realistic business priorities rather than theoretical perfection.

---

## 2.5 Potential Maturity Represents the Ceiling

Potential Maximum StackScore assumes every applicable recommendation has been completed.

It represents the theoretical maximum maturity—not a guarantee.

---

# 3. Maturity Models

StackScore calculates three independent maturity values.

---

## 3.1 Current StackScore

Represents the organization's actual technology maturity today.

Includes:

- Original assessment responses
- Completed recommendations
- Valid reassessments

Displayed as:

> **Current StackScore**

Example:

```
42
```

---

## 3.2 Planned StackScore

Represents expected maturity if all currently planned initiatives are completed.

Includes recommendations whose status is:

- Planned
- Quoted
- Approved
- In Progress
- Completed

Displayed as:

> **Planned StackScore**

Example:

```
81
```

---

## 3.3 Potential Maximum StackScore

Represents the highest achievable maturity assuming every applicable recommendation has been implemented.

Excludes:

- Deferred
- Rejected
- Not Applicable

Displayed as:

> **Potential Maximum StackScore**

Example:

```
100
```

---

# 4. Recommendation Lifecycle

Every recommendation exists in one lifecycle state.

| Status | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Open | Generated by assessment but not yet reviewed |
| Planned | Selected for future implementation |
| Quoted | Included in a customer proposal |
| Approved | Approved by the customer |
| In Progress | Actively being implemented |
| Completed | Successfully implemented |
| Deferred | Intentionally postponed |
| Rejected | Customer declined implementation |
| Not Applicable | Recommendation does not apply |

---

# 5. Score Contribution Rules

The Technology Maturity Engine shall calculate scores using the following rules.

| Recommendation Status | Current Score | Planned Score | Potential Maximum |
|----------------------|:-------------:|:-------------:|:----------------:|
| Open | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ |
| Planned | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Quoted | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Approved | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ |
| In Progress | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Completed | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Deferred | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ |
| Rejected | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ |
| Not Applicable | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ |

This table is considered the authoritative scoring model for StackScore.

# 6. Recommendation Metadata

Every recommendation shall contain the following information.

- Recommendation Title
- Business Priority
- Estimated StackScore Improvement
- Technology Pillar
- Business Impact
- Implementation Complexity
- Estimated Effort
- Estimated Cost *(Future Enhancement)*
- Associated Services
- Related Projects

---

# 7. Roadmap Progress

StackScore shall continuously calculate roadmap metrics.

These include:

- Total Recommendations
- Planned
- Quoted
- Approved
- In Progress
- Completed
- Deferred
- Rejected
- Remaining

These values should appear throughout executive dashboards and consultant dashboards.

# 9. Historical Maturity Tracking

Every recommendation reaching the **Completed** state shall generate a maturity history event.

Each event records:

- Completion Date
- Previous Score
- New Score
- Recommendation
- Implementing Consultant
- Associated Project
- Customer

Historical records enable:

- Quarterly Business Reviews
- Trend Charts
- Executive Reporting
- ROI Analysis
- Customer Progress Visualization

# 11. Design Goals

The Technology Maturity Engine shall ensure every customer can answer three questions at any point during their engagement.

## Where are we today?

Measured by:

**Current StackScore**

---

## Where are we going?

Measured by:

**Planned StackScore**

---

## What is ultimately possible?

Measured by:

**Potential Maximum StackScore**

---

By separating current performance, planned improvements, and theoretical maximum maturity, StackScore provides an accurate, transparent, and realistic representation of technology progress while reinforcing continuous improvement instead of one-time assessments.

---

# 12. Guiding Philosophy

Technology environments are never "finished."

As businesses evolve, technology must evolve alongside them.

StackScore exists to provide organizations with a measurable, transparent framework for continuous improvement—not simply a one-time report.

Success is not defined by reaching a score of 100.

Success is defined by making informed technology decisions, demonstrating measurable progress over time, reducing operational risk, and continuously improving business maturity.

The Technology Maturity Engine is the foundation that enables StackScore to become a long-term technology management platform rather than a static assessment tool.
