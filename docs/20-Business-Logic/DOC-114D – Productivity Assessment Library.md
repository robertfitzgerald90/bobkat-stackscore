# Purpose

The Productivity Assessment Library defines every assessment question used to evaluate how effectively technology enables employees to perform their work.

The objective is to identify technology inefficiencies, collaboration barriers, and process limitations that reduce productivity or create unnecessary operational overhead.

This document should be read in conjunction with:

* DOC-110 – Assessment Framework
* DOC-111 – Scoring Engine Specification
* DOC-112 – Recommendation Engine Specification
* DOC-114 – Assessment Library Specification

# Assessment Objective

The Productivity Assessment measures how well an organization's technology supports day-to-day business operations and employee efficiency.

Areas evaluated include:

* Microsoft 365 Utilization
* Collaboration Tools
* Communication Platforms
* File Management
* Mobile Workforce
* Device Experience
* Process Automation
* Business Applications
* User Experience

# Productivity Maturity Philosophy

Technology should simplify work, reduce manual effort, and improve collaboration.

A mature productivity environment enables employees to perform their jobs efficiently through standardized tools, streamlined workflows, and thoughtful technology adoption.

The assessment measures business enablement rather than the number of software products deployed.

# Business Rules

* Productivity questions shall evaluate business outcomes rather than software ownership.
* Technology adoption shall be considered alongside technology availability.
* Recommendations shall prioritize reducing manual effort and improving employee efficiency.
* Standardization should be favored over unnecessary complexity.
* All scoring shall be governed by DOC-111 – Scoring Engine Specification.
