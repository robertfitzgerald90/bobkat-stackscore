# 1. Executive Summary

## Overview

The Technology Standards & Catalog serves as the authoritative source of truth for every technology solution recognized, recommended, deployed, and supported within the StackScore platform.

Rather than functioning as a simple inventory of hardware and software, the Technology Standards & Catalog establishes the Bobkat IT Technology Standard by documenting approved vendors, products, implementation guidance, lifecycle information, supportability, and business value.

The Technology Standards & Catalog becomes the foundation for every recommendation generated by StackScore.

Recommendations, Roadmaps, Projects, Playbooks, Client Technology Profiles, Proposals, and future automation workflows should reference catalog entries instead of manually defining technologies.

Every technology recommendation within StackScore should originate from this catalog.

---

# Purpose

The Technology Standards & Catalog exists to:

- Establish the Bobkat IT Technology Standard.
- Standardize technology recommendations.
- Eliminate inconsistent consulting guidance.
- Improve proposal consistency.
- Improve implementation quality.
- Support repeatable consulting engagements.
- Provide reusable implementation knowledge.
- Create a scalable technology knowledge base.

---

# Product Vision

Technology consulting should be standardized wherever practical.

Rather than every consultant recommending products independently, StackScore should provide an approved catalog of technologies aligned with Bobkat IT consulting standards.

Each technology should explain:

- What it is.
- Why it exists.
- When it should be used.
- When it should not be used.
- How it should be implemented.
- How it integrates with other technologies.
- What business value it provides.

The catalog should become both a consulting reference and the intelligence layer behind StackScore.

---

Examples include:

# 2. Technology Standard Philosophy

## Overview

The Technology Standards & Catalog exists to define, maintain, and continuously improve the Bobkat IT Technology Standard.

Rather than functioning as a simple product catalog, the Technology Standards & Catalog establishes the technologies, platforms, vendors, and implementation methodologies that Bobkat IT recommends when designing, deploying, and supporting customer environments.

Every technology recommendation generated by StackScore should be rooted in this standard.

The catalog represents years of consulting experience translated into a repeatable framework that ensures customers receive consistent, high-quality technology guidance regardless of who performs the assessment or implementation.

---

# Core Philosophy

Technology should solve business problems.

Products should never be recommended simply because they are popular, inexpensive, or familiar.

Every technology selected by Bobkat IT should satisfy one or more measurable business objectives.

Examples include:

- Improve Security
- Improve Reliability
- Improve Visibility
- Improve Manageability
- Reduce Operational Complexity
- Reduce Business Risk
- Improve Scalability
- Improve User Experience
- Reduce Long-Term Cost of Ownership

Technology exists to enable business success.

---

# Standardization Philosophy

Standardization is one of the most effective methods for reducing operational complexity.

By deploying consistent technologies across customer environments, Bobkat IT can:

- Improve support quality.
- Reduce troubleshooting time.
- Improve documentation consistency.
- Simplify training.
- Improve automation.
- Improve disaster recovery.
- Increase implementation quality.
- Deliver more predictable customer outcomes.

Whenever practical, Bobkat IT should recommend technologies already included within the Technology Standard.

Exceptions should be intentional and documented.

---

# Vendor Selection Philosophy

Bobkat IT should maintain a curated list of approved vendors.

Vendors are selected based on:

- Product quality
- Reliability
- Security
- Support experience
- Product maturity
- Long-term viability
- Integration capabilities
- Ease of management
- Value delivered to customers

No vendor should remain part of the standard solely because of familiarity or historical use.

Technology Standards should evolve as better solutions emerge.

- What operational risk exists?
- What business objective is being supported?

- What operational burden is reduced?

Products are selected only after these questions have been answered.
