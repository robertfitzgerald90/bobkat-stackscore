# Purpose

The Executive Business Review (EBR) provides a structured executive-level review of the client's Technology Program.

Rather than reviewing isolated projects or technical work, the Executive Business Review demonstrates measurable business progress, validates technology investments, communicates strategic direction, and reinforces the long-term partnership between the consultant and the client.

The Executive Business Review is intended to become the primary recurring meeting within the client relationship.

---

# Vision

Technology consulting should not revolve around fixing problems.

Technology consulting should demonstrate continuous business improvement.

The Executive Business Review exists to answer one question:

**"How has technology improved this business since our last meeting?"**

Every report, dashboard, project, assessment, and roadmap should contribute toward answering that question.

---

# Philosophy

Clients invest in outcomes.

Not labor.

Not tickets.

Not recommendations.

Every Executive Business Review should demonstrate measurable value created since the previous review.

Technology should be presented as a strategic business investment rather than operational expense.

---

# Objectives

The Executive Business Review exists to:

- Demonstrate measurable progress.
- Reinforce consulting value.
- Review Technology Program health.
- Validate completed investments.
- Review business outcomes.
- Align future priorities.
- Build executive confidence.
- Strengthen long-term partnerships.

---

# Review Frequency

Recommended cadence:

Quarterly

Additional reviews may occur:

- Annual Strategic Review
- Major Project Completion
- Annual Assessment
- Executive Planning Sessions

The cadence should support continuous improvement rather than reactive consulting.

# Standard Review Structure

## Executive Summary

High-level overview of organizational progress.

---

## Technology Program Health

Examples:

- Overall Technology Score
- Technology Program Completion
- Current Phase
- Immediate Focus
- Technology Health

---

## Technology Journey

Review milestones achieved since the previous Executive Business Review.

Examples:

- Completed Projects
- Major Assessments
- Strategic Milestones
- Business Outcomes

---

## Project Review

Review Projects completed during the period.

Each Project should summarize:

- Business Objective
- Deliverables
- Technology Improvements
- Business Outcomes
- Executive Summary

Projects should demonstrate value—not activity.

---

## Investment Summary

Technology investments made during the review period.

Examples:

- Hardware
- Software
- Licensing
- Professional Services
- Consulting
- Managed Services

Investment should always be paired with delivered value.

---

## Business Outcomes

Examples:

- Reduced Cyber Risk
- Increased Productivity
- Reduced Downtime
- Improved Disaster Recovery
- Improved Documentation
- Improved Compliance
- Standardized Infrastructure

Business outcomes should remain the primary focus.

---

## Risk Review

Current organizational risk profile.

Examples:

Previous Risk

↓

Current Risk

Review:

- Critical Risks
- Emerging Risks
- Remaining Risks

Risk trends should be emphasized.

---

## Roadmap Progress

Current Technology Investment Roadmap.

Examples:

Completed Phase

Current Phase

Upcoming Phase

Immediate Focus

Expected Investments

The Roadmap should reinforce long-term planning.

---

## Strategic Discussion

Consultant-led discussion.

Topics may include:

- Business growth
- Budget
- Staffing
- Technology priorities
- Organizational change
- Industry trends
- Future planning

The Executive Business Review should encourage executive conversation—not presentation.
